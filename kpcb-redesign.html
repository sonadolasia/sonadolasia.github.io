Nextdoor

Conceptual Re-design - January 2019

For my application to the KPCB Design Fellowship, I chose to re-design Nextdoor, a social network for neighborhoods. Nextdoor is a popular platform in my community for communication and recommendations, and I wanted to investigate ways that the design could potentially be improved.

USER RESEARCH 

Through my user research I first wanted to understand how people use Nextdoor's mobile application. I played around with the platform myself, and I discovered that there was a lot of different features with the product. 

  1. What are the main uses for Nextdoor?
  2. What are the pain points of the platform?

Usability Testing on Exisiting Application 

The first step of my research was asking people who had never used the Nextdoor app to explore and then do a series of tasks. I wanted to understand how easy the app is to use and if its many different features are obvious

User Survey

I also sent out a survey to Nextdoor users, by posting on the application itself. I wanted to userstand what users thought about the mobile app vs the website and what features they find most important.  

KEY INSIGHTS

  1. Navigation: Organization of posts is confusing and hard to navigate, only one place to search
  2. Inconsistant Wording : Some of the icons have common associations different than the ones on the platform. 
  3. Time: Finding saved posts is difficult. 

IDEATION

My user research demonstrated that the most pain points were with the mobile application of Nextdoor, so I decided to focus my re-design on that platform. 

PAIN POINTS AND POTENTIAL SOLUTIONS

I began by jotting down the main painpoints I found in my user research, and began ideating solutions for those. 

CURRENT UI

I then looked at the current UI of the application and began sketching out a redeisgn of it.
