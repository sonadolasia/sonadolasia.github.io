ubiquity press

simplifying the conference planning process

s/o berkeley innovation

details

team: sona dolasia + mattin delvar + ellen nguyen + shirley sun + warren lai (pm) 

role: user reseach lead

timeline: jan - may 2019

deliverables: high fidelity prototypes in figma, user research report, competitive analysis

context

as a product design consultant for berkeley innovation, my team worked with british academic publisher, ubiquity press. the company was devloping a conference planning platform to help academic conferences manage submitted abstract/papers.

the goals for this project were to: conduct user research, redesign/refine existing features, and add functionalities to complete the product.

research

🎤 interviews

spoke with 9 organizers and attendees to learn more about their needs.

📚 secondary research

researched 11 exisiting platforms. noted pros, cons, usability, and other features.

📝 surveys

sent to organizers and attendees, to gain better insight into current practices and painpoints.

insights

🚤 streamlining

Information needed to create a conference is presented in an unorganized and lengthy way.

🎨 customizationn

Organizers want to be able to customize their conference site to include various features and implement their branding.

📆 scheduling

Organizers need a simple and efficient way to schedule events at conferences. Attendees want to personalize their own schedule by interest.

🔆 highlight details

Key information about conferences is often hard to find. Attendees want to easily be able to see information about location, dates, deadlines, etc. quickly.

🛠 multifunctional

Organizers want a platform that supports all aspects of running a conference (submissions, website, ticketing, etc).

reflection

this was my first project in berkeley innovation, and my first project doing work for a client. i learned so much from my team and our client about the design process.

looking back, i wish we had time to conduct more user testing with the high fidelity screens. since there were only a number of screens we designed up, it was difficult to get accurate testing on the navigation improvements, given there were only a few pages to choose from.
